Gaming History Timeline

1972
Atari released Pong, video game mania ensues.
1977
Atari unveils the Atari 2600, which housed favorites such as “Space Invaders”, “Donkey Kong”, and “Frogger”.
1983
Based off the popular movie of the time, E.T is released for the Atari 2600. Dubbed one of the worst video games of all time. the company behind said game ended up burying thousands of copies in the New Mexico desert. Story Here
1985
The Nintendo Entertainment System is released, mass adoption as the general population seeks to partake in video gaming in the household. Introduces many cult classics such as Super Mario Bros.”, “The Legend of Zelda” and “Final Fantasy.
1989
Sega Genesis is launched, a new more powerful console when compared to NES, and housed many titles with less of a focus on family-friendly. Brought about fan favorites like "Sonic the Hedgehog".
1991
Nintendo releases the Super Nintendo Entertainment System, a new upgraded console that could output more colors on screen than the Sega Genesis. Popular titles launched were "Donkey Kong Country", "Super Mario World" and "Star Fox".
1989

1995
Windows released Windows 95, with more accessible and powerful PC options available, PC gaming really starts to become popular. Games such as "Quake", "MDK", "Fallout"...the list could go on here.
1996
Nintendo releases their first 3D-Capable console, the Nintendo 64. One of my favorites, this console launched and created many popular titles such as "Super Mario 64", "Pokemon Stadium" and "Glover".
